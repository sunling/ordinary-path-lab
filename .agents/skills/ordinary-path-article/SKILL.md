# Ordinary Path Article

This skill helps turn Ordinary Path Lab materials into articles with the user's personal style: grounded, specific, honest about constraints, and rooted in real problems.

## Core Position

Ordinary Path Lab articles are not generic career advice. They should feel like the user is thinking with a real person in front of her.

The tone must never become preachy. The article should not sound like the writer is standing above the reader, correcting them, educating them, or delivering a life lesson.

Write from:

- Real questions before abstract conclusions.
- Concrete situations before frameworks.
- Judgment process before confident prescription.
- Ordinary resources, constraints, and small actions before success narratives.
- A warm but clear personal voice before polished institutional language.
- Shared thinking before instruction.

## Source Order

When drafting from repository materials, read in this order:

1. `README.md` for project scope and workflow.
2. `SOUL.md` for the Sun Ling perspective, voice, and non-preachy writing stance.
3. `05-outputs/visual-system.md` when the task includes cover images, inline illustrations, or publish-ready article packaging.
4. The relevant `03-issue-units/` file if the article answers one reusable question.
5. The relevant `02-normalized/` records to recover real context, phrasing, and boundaries.
6. The relevant `04-topic-clusters/` file if the article needs a broader theme.
7. `05-outputs/content-backlog.md`, `service-offers.md`, or `connection-pool.md` only when the article is tied to outputs.
8. Existing drafts in `06-article-drafts/` when revising or continuing an article.

Do not write only from `04-topic-clusters` or `05-outputs` if the article claims to come from lived consultation material. Go back to the original normalized records.

## Article Shape

Prefer this structure unless the task says otherwise:

1. Open with a concrete question, scene, or tension from the material.
2. Name the real problem behind the surface question.
3. Explain the user's judgment process: what seems possible, what is risky, what cannot be promised.
4. Offer a few grounded directions or small next steps.
5. Close simply, without forced elevation.

For series articles, keep the series introduction short. One or two plain paragraphs are enough:

- Say this is part of a series整理常被问到的问题.
- Mention the questions come from readers, consultations, chats, or concrete life choices.
- Do not turn the opening into a project manifesto.
- Move quickly into the specific question for this article.

When a public article needs a question-collection ending, use a light invitation rather than marketing language. The current default ending can mention:

- The user is collecting "我常被问到的 100 个问题".
- Readers can reply with the keyword `提问` in the WeChat official account backend to submit a question.
- Publicly usable questions may be anonymized and turned into future articles.
- More specific questions can be handled through one-on-one consultation.

Keep this invitation short and grounded; avoid urgency, scarcity, sales pressure, or exaggerated value claims.

## Article Length

For public-facing essays in this series, aim for about 3000 Chinese characters unless the user asks otherwise.

When shortening:

- Preserve the real question, the judgment path, and the concrete next step.
- Cut repeated transitions, over-explanation, and generic setup.
- Merge adjacent paragraphs only when readability stays natural.
- Do not remove privacy boundaries or uncertainty caveats just to meet length.
- If frontmatter is present, count the body separately from frontmatter when reporting length.

## Visual Assets

When the user asks for publish-ready article packaging, or asks to generate visuals while drafting an article, use `05-outputs/visual-system.md`.

Default visual workflow:

1. Create or update the article's visual record in `05-outputs/visual-system.md`.
2. Generate a cover background image without Chinese title text.
3. Save the selected cover background under `05-outputs/article-visuals/`.
4. Provide the title text separately for later overlay.
5. Generate inline illustrations only when they clarify structure, experiments, or decision criteria.

For the "我常被问到的 100 个问题" series:

- Use a consistent cover system with article number, series title, and short article title.
- Avoid asking AI image generation to render full Chinese titles; generate a text-free background and overlay Chinese later.
- Keep visuals quiet, realistic, restrained, and non-commercial.
- Avoid robots, cyberpunk, course-ad style, success imagery, and exaggerated tech glow.

For inline visuals, prefer simple note/card/diagram styles:

- Concept split: e.g. `工具 / 协作能力 / 职业转型`.
- Small experiment card.
- Reality-condition checklist.

Good article energy:

- "我会先看这个人真实站在哪里。"
- "这件事不是不能做，但不能只靠一个愿望来做。"
- "我更关心的是，她手里现在有什么资源，又被什么东西卡住。"
- "这不是一个标准答案，而是一种判断顺序。"

Avoid:

- Generic openings like "随着时代的发展" or "在当今社会".
- Overconfident promises about school admission, visas, job markets, salary, or life outcomes.
- Treating one person's case as a universal rule.
- Excessive frameworks, numbered slogans, or AI-flavored summaries.
- Turning painful personal material into inspirational content.
- Preachy phrasing that tells readers what they "should" do with their life.
- High-ground evaluative phrasing like "这个想法不荒唐", "这里不是为了吓退谁", or "你需要清醒一点".

## Personal Voice Rules

The final Chinese should sound natural, concrete, and lightly reflective.

Use:

- Short to medium paragraphs.
- Plain Chinese over unnecessary English.
- Specific nouns from the case after anonymization.
- "我会..." and "我更倾向于..." when the article is clearly the user's judgment.
- Uncertainty when the issue is genuinely uncertain.
- Observations and questions that invite the reader to think along.

Reduce:

- "赋能", "底层逻辑", "闭环", "抓手", "赛道", "认知升级".
- Repeated "首先/其次/最后" unless the article is explicitly a checklist.
- Over-neat conclusions.
- Moral judgment toward the person asking.
- Sentences that evaluate the asker from above, even when meant kindly.
- "你应该", "你必须", "一定要", "只要你...", "真正成熟的人..." and similar teacherly or motivational phrasing.

## Non-Preachy Tone

Replace instruction-from-above with thinking-from-beside.

Prefer:

- "我会先把这件事拆小一点看。"
- "对这个人来说，可能更现实的问题不是要不要转码，而是她现在能不能低成本验证。"
- "这里我不会直接劝她冲，也不会直接劝她放弃。"
- "如果是我来判断，我会先看三个东西。"

Avoid:

- "年轻人一定要尽早认清现实。"
- "你必须为自己的人生负责。"
- "不要再逃避了。"
- "真正厉害的人都懂得长期主义。"
- "只要坚持学习，普通人也能逆袭。"

When revising, scan for sentences that sound like advice to a faceless crowd. Convert them into context-aware judgment, first-person reflection, or a question tied to the case.

Also scan for sentences that reassure or warn from a higher position. Prefer naming the real conditions directly: money, time, identity, family, body, information, risk, and uncertainty.

## Privacy Rules

Default assumption: all consultation material is private.

Before publishing or marking a draft as public-ready, remove or blur:

- Real names, handles, emails, phone numbers, WeChat IDs.
- Specific cities, schools, companies, projects, exact salaries, debts, visa status.
- Family conflict, domestic violence, depression, bereavement, medical details, pregnancy, and other high-sensitivity life events.
- Commercial plans, quotes, cooperation details, or unpublished projects.

For small-group or connection-pool content, do not expose identity details. Record concrete contact and consent status only in `07-people/`.

## Verification Rules

If the article mentions policies, school rules, tuition, visa status, admissions requirements, job markets, or other time-sensitive facts, do not present them as stable fact from memory. Either:

- Keep them as personal experience and judgment logic, or
- Verify against official/current sources before publication.

When unsure, say what is uncertain and what the reader should verify themselves.

## Drafting Workflow

When asked to create an article:

1. Identify the exact question the article answers.
2. Read `SOUL.md` before shaping the voice.
3. Read the relevant issue unit and normalized records.
4. Decide what can be public, what must be anonymized, and what should be omitted.
5. Draft in `06-article-drafts/` unless the user provides another target.
6. Do a full readthrough for voice, privacy, and factual overclaiming.

When asked to revise an article:

1. Read the whole draft before editing.
2. Preserve good personal details and judgment process.
3. Cut generic advice, AI-flavored transitions, and forced takeaways.
4. Add context only if it is grounded in source records.
5. Remove preachy or high-ground evaluative sentences and rewrite them as grounded observations.
6. Check privacy again after editing.

## Final Checklist

Before finishing, verify:

- The article answers one clear real question.
- The article reflects `SOUL.md` without turning Sun Ling into an all-knowing authority.
- It keeps the user's personal judgment and tone.
- It includes concrete situations without exposing private identity.
- It does not turn individual experience into guaranteed advice.
- It does not sound preachy, superior, high-ground, motivational, or teacherly.
- It has no generic AI-style opening or ending.
- Time-sensitive claims are either verified or clearly framed as experience.
